Partnering with TAFEs to address skills needs
La Trobe enjoys long-standing and close partnerships with Victorian TAFEs. With our regional TAFE
partners, we have established the Northern Victorian Education Partnership to increase participation
in post-school education across northern Victoria and respond to regional workforce needs.
We are partnering to deliver Suburban Study Hub facilities at Melbourne Polytechnic's Epping campus
and opposite Bendigo Kangan Institute's campus in Broadmeadows, to enable more students to participate
and succeed in higher education.
La Trobe and Kangan Institute are also working together to innovate health and care training in Melbourne's
north, combining La Trobe's expertise with state-of-the-art facilities at the Kangan Institute's new
Health and Community Centre in Broadmeadows.
We will explore further opportunities to collaborate with TAFE partners to support the tertiary education
needs of Melbourne's northern growth corridor, and to provide innovative lifelong learning courses.

Thought leadership and public engagement
Our university has a proud tradition of thought leadership and public engagement, with La Trobe
producing some of Australia's finest public intellectuals, leading and influencing discussion, and
debate on issues of national and global relevance. In 2022, we created an Australian-first role of
'Professor of Public Engagement' to help strengthen this foundation and build capacity within
our academic community to advance engaged scholarship.
We will continue to stimulate discussion and debate on critical policy and social issues through
public events, commentary and exhibitions, and by publishing high-quality scholarship for a
general audience through La Trobe University Press.
Our research centres will inform evidence-based policy development in health, education, agriculture,
technology, and security. Our social science, humanities, and legal experts will play a vital role in building
public understanding of contemporary society, including on social justice issues, technological
advancements, and geopolitical shifts that are rapidly reshaping the world.
Through the work of the La Trobe Art Institute and our strategic partnerships with cultural institutions,
we will make a dynamic contribution to the intellectual and cultural life of the communities we serve.

AI and medical innovation
The Australian Centre for AI in Medical Innovation, launched in 2024 and powered by a dedicated
Nvidia DGX H200 supercomputer, will use state-of-the-art high performance AI computing to support
medical research institutes, health organisations, and industry through research and workforce
development, ensuring Victoria's world-leading medical research ecosystem maintains pace with global
standards and opportunities as AI capabilities develop. Together with BioNTech's new clinical-scale
manufacturing mRNA facility at our Bundoora campus, the Olivia Newton John Cancer Research
Institute, and the La Trobe Institute for Molecular Science, the University is creating an ecosystem in
Melbourne's north to drive innovation in therapeutic treatments to fight cancer and other diseases.
